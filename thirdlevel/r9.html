What is this book about?


I bought a copy of Biggles Adventure Double and have read one of the adventures called “Biggles the Camels are Coming”. It is a story about teenage pilot called James Bigglesworth A.K.A Biggles. The ‘Camels’ of the title are not animals but Sopwith F1 Camels, a British World War 1 fighter plane. Biggles is an air force Captain although he is only 17 or 18 during the time when the story takes place. This is a very exciting story. Each chapter contains a thrilling episode where Biggles’ courage, flying skills and cunning help him to overcome enemy German fighter planes.
Some of the chapters contain very exciting, heart stopping descriptions of “dog fights” where Biggles and his friends are in peril but somehow manage to survive.
Biggles is not just some boy hero adventure story. It is clear that the strain of the war, the death of his friends has great emotional impact on Biggles. By the end of the book he is described by his Commanding Officer of “drinking whisky for breakfast” and is under such strain that he doesn’t care if he lives or dies.




What did you particularly enjoy about this book?

I liked the amount of action that was in this book. Every chapter is so exciting. Once you start a chapter it is very difficult to not finish it, as you will really want to know how Biggles manged to overcome whatever dangerous situation he found himself in.
I really like Biggles as a character. He is brave and honest and clever. The fact that he is so young, under so much strain but still manages to outwit his enemies makes reading about his adventures so engrossing.


What did you dislike about this book?


Nothing.


In summary, who do you think might like this book? Is there a particular age range it’s best suited to? Are there other books or series you think it’s similar to?


Readers interested in war stories and adventure stories with lots of action and fighting will enjoy Biggles.
