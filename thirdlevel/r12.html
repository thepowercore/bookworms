What is this book about?


The Eagle in the Snow is a story about Barney, a boy whose house was destroyed by the German bombing of Coventry during World War 2. Leaving the ruins of Coventry behind on a train to London with his mother, Barney meets a stranger who has an incredible, dramatic and enthralling story to tell.
The stranger’s story is about a friend, a brave soldier fighting in World War 1, who makes a split second decision which will have consequences that he could never have imagined.
The stranger tells Barney most of the story while they are stuck on the train in a tunnel because a German plane has attacked the train. The atmosphere in the train compartment is intense and the stranger’s story has lots of action and lots of twists.




What did you particularly enjoy about this book?

I like books with a lots of action and fighting like The Eagle in the Snow. The plot of the book has many twists and I found that I couldn’t stop reading it once I has started. Billy, whose story the stranger on the train told, was my favourite character. I liked Billy because he was a very brave and heroic soldier who makes a terrible mistake. The reader can really feel how upset Billy is about the terrible decision he made during World War 1.


What did you dislike about this book?


While I really liked The Eagle in the Snow I thought that some of the story was rushed and that such a good story deserved a longer book.


In summary, who do you think might like this book? Is there a particular age range it’s best suited to? Are there other books or series you think it’s similar to?


The Eagle in the Snow will appeal to readers who like a really good suspense-filled story and will appeal to readers who like war stories in particular.
