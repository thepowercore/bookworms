What is this book about?


This is a story told from a number of viewpoints about a greyhound that was initially saved from drowning by a boy called Patrick. The story takes us through the life of the greyhound from there with his many consecutive owners, both good and bad until he finally finds happiness and contentment.
While the story has many exciting twists, which keep the reader interested, the book is mainly about friendship.




What did you particularly enjoy about this book?

I like the book ‘Born to Run’ because it is very exciting. Each chapter introduces unexpected events. I love the bond between the dog and his many owners and the friendship and loyalty that they share. The dog helps the owners to deal with their own challenges as he fits himself into their daily lives. The dog is adaptable, loyal and intelligent and when you read the book you really care about how things will work out for him.


What did you dislike about this book?


The book is quite sad in parts. While the sad parts of the book add to the overall story they are a bit upsetting to read.


In summary, who do you think might like this book? Is there a particular age range it’s best suited to? Are there other books or series you think it’s similar to?


While this book is not part of a series, many of Michael Morpurgo feature animals as central characters, so a reader who enjoyed ‘Born to Run’ may also enjoy other of Morpurgo's books.
